* Overview

This package provides utilities for working with Walmart's fiscal calendar system. Walmart uses a custom week-based fiscal calendar that differs from standard calendar weeks.

* Walmart Fiscal Calendar Rules

** Week Definition
- A Walmart week runs from *Saturday to Friday* (7 days)
- Week 1 of each fiscal year starts on the *Saturday on or before February 1st*
- If February 1st falls on a Saturday, that is the start of Week 1
- If February 1st falls on any other day, Week 1 starts on the Saturday immediately before it

** Fiscal Year Naming
- Fiscal years are named after the *calendar year in which they end*
- Example: FY2025 starts around late January/early February 2024 and ends in January 2025
- Most fiscal years have 52 weeks, but occasionally have 53 weeks

** Examples
- *2024*: February 1 was a Thursday → Week 1 started on Saturday, January 27, 2024 (FY2025)
- *2025*: February 1 is a Saturday → Week 1 starts on Saturday, February 1, 2025 (FY2026)
- *2026*: February 1 is a Sunday → Week 1 starts on Saturday, January 31, 2026 (FY2027)

* API Documentation

** Primary Function: ~walmart-week~

#+begin_src emacs-lisp
(walmart-week &optional date)
#+end_src

*** Description
Returns the Walmart week number and fiscal year for a given date.

*** Parameters
- ~date~ (optional): A list =(YEAR MONTH DAY)=
  - If omitted, uses the current date
  - Example: ~'(2024 6 15)~ for June 15, 2024

*** Returns
A cons cell ~(WEEK-NUMBER . FISCAL-YEAR)~
- ~WEEK-NUMBER~: Integer from 1 to 53
- ~FISCAL-YEAR~: Integer representing the fiscal year (e.g., 2025)

*** Examples

#+begin_src emacs-lisp
;; Get current Walmart week
(walmart-week)
;; => (21 . 2025)

;; Get week for specific date
(walmart-week '(2024 6 15))
;; => (21 . 2025)

;; Get week for Feb 1, 2025 (Saturday - Week 1 start)
(walmart-week '(2025 2 1))
;; => (1 . 2026)
#+end_src

* Implementation Details

** Date-Based Approach
The implementation uses a simplified date-based approach:
- Works with day numbers (integers) rather than time values
- Uses Emacs' ~time-to-days~ function for date arithmetic
- Avoids floating-point arithmetic and DST edge cases
- Daylight Saving Time transitions are not an issue since:
  - DST starts and ends on Sundays
  - Walmart weeks start and end on Saturdays

** Key Algorithm
1. Convert target date to a day number
2. Find the Saturday on or before February 1st for candidate years
3. Select the most recent Week 1 start that precedes the target date
4. Calculate week number by dividing days elapsed by 7

** Display Current Week in Modeline
#+begin_src emacs-lisp
(defun my-walmart-week-modeline ()
  "Return Walmart week string for modeline."
  (let* ((result (walmart-week))
         (week (car result))
         (fy (cdr result)))
    (format "WW%d" week)))
#+end_src

** Calculate Week for Date Range
#+begin_src emacs-lisp
(defun walmart-weeks-in-range (start-date end-date)
  "Get all unique Walmart weeks between START-DATE and END-DATE."
  (let ((weeks '())
        (current-date start-date))
    (while (not (time-less-p end-date current-date))
      (let ((week (walmart-week current-date)))
        (unless (member week weeks)
          (push week weeks)))
      ;; Move to next day
      (setq current-date 
            (time-add current-date (days-to-time 1))))
    (nreverse weeks)))
#+end_src
